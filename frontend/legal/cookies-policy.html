Cookies Policy

Last updated: January 22, 2026

This Cookies Policy explains how AI Prompt Generator ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website. This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and give website owners useful information about how their site is being used.

Similar technologies include:

  • Local Storage: Data stored in your browser that persists even after you close it
  • Session Storage: Data stored temporarily during your browsing session
  • Web Beacons: Small graphics that track user behavior

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential Functions: To make our website work correctly
  • Preferences: To remember your settings (like dark mode or language)
  • Analytics: To understand how visitors use our website
  • Security: To help protect against abuse and maintain security

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They cannot be disabled in our systems. They are usually set in response to actions you take, such as setting your privacy preferences.

3.2 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization, such as remembering your preferences (theme, language). If you don't allow these cookies, some features may not work properly.

3.3 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This helps us improve our website and services.

5. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are placed by third-party services that appear on our pages:

5.1 Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to analyze how visitors use our website. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect information about:

  • How you arrived at our website
  • Pages you visited and how long you spent on them
  • Your approximate geographic location (country/city level)
  • Device and browser information

Google Analytics data is anonymized (IP anonymization is enabled). For more information, see Google's Privacy Policy.

6. Managing Cookies

6.1 Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie consent banner. You can:

  • Accept: Allow all cookies including analytics
  • Decline: Only essential cookies will be used

You can change your preference at any time by clearing your browser cookies and revisiting our website.

6.2 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • See what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Clear all cookies when you close the browser

Here are links to cookie settings for popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

6.3 Opting Out of Google Analytics

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

7. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable cookies, some features of our website may not function properly:

  • Your theme preference (dark/light mode) won't be remembered
  • Your language preference won't be saved
  • You may need to accept the cookie consent banner on each visit

The core functionality of generating prompts will still work without cookies.

8. Do Not Track

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that signals to websites that you don't want to be tracked. We respect these signals and will disable analytics tracking when this setting is enabled.
